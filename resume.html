Résumé

Summary

Blithe Rocher is a self-taught developer who has been building web applications for over a year. She started by crafting her personal website and became interested in taking her skills to the next level.

Early on, Blithe developed her skills while giving back as a beta tester for Codecademy. She has participated in Rails Girls Atlanta and the Global Day of Code Retreat. She spends most of her free time hacking on personal projects and working to become a professional web developer.

Blithe is interested in a junior-level position with a development team of smart people where she can contribute her talents and enthusiasm for learning to make cool things.

Current Projects

Dog Walker App

Building a database-driven web application (source) with Ruby on Rails that helps dog owners find others to walk their dogs. The app has test coverage using RSpec and Capybara and is backed by a PostgreSQL database. It is deployed on Heroku.

Learn Ruby Gem

Collaborating with Tom Black to develop a gem and a tutorial to help anyone learn Ruby.
